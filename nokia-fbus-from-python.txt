So yesterday I found <a href="http://www.embedtronics.com/nokia/fbus.html">this</a> article and I wanted to try it out from Python.

Here is a few lines of code to send that "get hw and firmware version" message to the phone and at least read an ACK:

<code>
import serial, time

ser = serial.Serial("/dev/ttyUSB0", 115200)
ser.setRTS(False)
ser.write("\x1E\x00\x0C\xD1\x00\x07\x00\x01\x00\x03\x00\x01\x40\x00\x52\xD5")
print repr(ser.read(1))
print repr(ser.read(10))
</code>

Of course this is just a PoC, the first read could be avoided and we could just read one char till we hit the 0x1e one. But that .setRTS() took a few hours to figure out. ;-)
